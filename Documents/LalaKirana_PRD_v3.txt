4. Dynamic Pricing System
Many products — especially grains, spices, and cooking oil — have prices that change daily based on market rates. LalaKirana supports instant price changes at any time with full auditability.

4.1  How Price Changes Work
Price Change Behaviour
•  Any product's price can be updated at any time from the product edit screen
•  The new price takes effect immediately for all new bills from that moment
•  All past bills are completely unaffected — bill_items stores a price snapshot at time of sale
•  A price history log records every change: old price, new price, who changed it, when
•  The billing screen shows 'Last updated: 6h ago' next to each product's price
•  If a price is over 12 hours old, a yellow warning badge appears on the billing screen

4.2  Bulk Morning Price Update Screen
Instead of editing products one by one, the operator opens the Bulk Price Update screen every morning and updates all changed prices in a single session.

Bulk Price Update — How It Works
•  Screen shows all products in a scrollable list with their current price in an editable field
•  Operator changes only the prices that are different today — unchanged fields are skipped
•  One 'Save All Changes' button applies all updates atomically
•  System logs each changed price in price_history with timestamp and previous value
•  After save: billing screen immediately reflects new prices across all order slots
•  Estimated time for a full price update (50–100 products): under 2 minutes

5. End-of-Day (EOD) Stock Entry
Small cash sales under ₹150 are handled verbally or noted in a paper diary during rush hour. At the end of each day (or start of next morning), the operator enters the diary totals into LalaKirana in a single batch session. This keeps stock accurate without slowing down service.

5.1  The Hybrid Stock Strategy
Sale Type
During Rush Hour
Stock Deduction
Large sale / khata / ₹150+
Full Bill — product selected
Immediate on confirmation
Small cash sale <₹150
Verbal or paper diary entry
EOD batch entry — same night or next morning
Quick Bill (any amount)
Type amount only, no product
EOD entry or monthly audit fills the gap

5.2  EOD Stock Entry Screen — Behaviour
How the EOD Stock Entry Screen Works
•  Simple two-column screen: product name (searchable) | quantity sold today
•  Operator reads from paper diary and enters qty sold for each item
•  Products not sold today are left blank — zero entries are skipped
•  One 'Confirm Entry' button deducts all quantities from stock in one transaction
•  Each deduction logged in stock_log with reason='eod_entry' and today's date
•  No bill number generated — this is a stock correction, not a sales bill
•  Total value of EOD entries shown as a summary for cross-reference with cash in hand
•  Operator can save a draft mid-entry and return to it later

5.3  EOD Entry vs Monthly Audit
With daily EOD entries, the monthly stock audit becomes a quick verification rather than a full count. Expected variance drops to near zero for most products.
Without EOD Entry
With EOD Entry
Monthly audit takes hours — full physical count
Monthly audit is a 15-minute spot-check
Large variance from Quick Bill gap
Near-zero variance — diary items entered daily
Stock levels unreliable during the month
Stock levels accurate by next morning every day
Hard to know what sold vs what was stolen/damaged
Daily entries separate sales from shrinkage clearly

8. User Stories

US-01   Priority: High
As a shop operator, I want to manage 6–8 open orders simultaneously on one screen
so that rush hour is handled without paper slips or missed orders

US-02   Priority: High
As a shop operator, I want to update all changed product prices in one screen every morning
so that the entire price update takes under 2 minutes and goes live immediately

US-03   Priority: High
As a shop operator, I want to see a stale price warning on the billing screen
so that I never accidentally bill a customer at yesterday's price

US-04   Priority: High
As a shop operator, I want to enter today's paper diary items in one EOD batch
so that stock levels are accurate by next morning without slowing down rush hour

US-05   Priority: High
As a shop operator, I want to add a purchase to a customer's khata
so that credit customers are tracked digitally without a physical diary

US-06   Priority: High
As a shop owner, I want to reset my own password or any staff password
so that I'm never locked out of the system

US-07   Priority: High
As a shop owner, I want to see all active login sessions and log any of them out
so that if a device is lost or a staff member leaves, I can secure the system immediately

US-08   Priority: High
As a shop operator, I want to continue billing when WiFi drops
so that a connectivity issue never stops me from serving customers

US-09   Priority: High
As a shop operator, I want to see a warning when prices changed while I was offline
so that I don't accidentally bill at stale offline-cached prices after reconnecting

US-10   Priority: Medium
As a shop owner, I want to view the price change history for any product
so that I can audit who changed what price and when
