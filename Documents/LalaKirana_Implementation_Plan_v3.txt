2. The Real Problem at Rush Hour
The shop has enough staff to take orders verbally from 6–8 customers at once. The bottleneck is the single computer. Staff away from the computer write orders on paper and hand them to the computer operator. This creates a handoff problem — slips get lost, quantities are misread, and the computer operator gets overwhelmed.

LalaKirana solves this with an Order Queue Manager built into the main billing screen.

2.1  How the Order Queue Manager Works
Think of it like a restaurant kitchen display — all active orders visible on one screen, the operator switches between them with a single click.

Order Queue Manager — Key Behaviour
•  Left sidebar shows all active orders as named slots: 'Order 1 — Ramesh', 'Order 2 — Unknown', etc.
•  Staff at the counter calls out a customer name or number — operator clicks that slot and starts adding items
•  Other orders stay exactly as they were — switching slots never clears or loses data
•  Each order shows its current total at a glance in the sidebar
•  Status badge on each slot: Draft / Ready to Pay / Paid / Khata (credit)
•  Operator marks an order Paid when customer pays — it moves to history automatically
•  Orders taken on paper by other staff are entered by the operator between serving customers
•  New order slots can be created instantly with a single button click

3. The Khata System (Digital Shop Diary)
Many customers come to the shop on credit — they take goods and pay at the end of the month or in partial instalments. Currently this is tracked in a physical diary. LalaKirana replaces this with a fully digital Khata ledger.

3.2  How a Khata Transaction Works
When a customer wants goods on credit, the operator handles it in 3 steps:

Select or create the customer's Khata profile (search by name or phone)
Build the bill normally — add all items, verify total
Instead of marking Paid, select 'Add to Khata' — bill is saved and balance updated

When the customer comes to repay (monthly or partial):

Open their Khata profile — current balance shown prominently
Enter the repayment amount
System logs the payment and updates the remaining balance instantly
Print a repayment receipt for the customer if needed

3.3  The Monthly Statement
What the Printable Statement Includes
•  Customer name and contact details at the top
•  Table of all purchases during the month — date, items, amount per visit
•  Table of all repayments made during the month
•  Opening balance (what they owed at start of month)
•  Closing balance (what they still owe)
•  Your shop name, address, and date of statement

This replaces the physical diary they bring to the shop. They can verify it against the statement. No disputes about what was written.

Important Note on Stock
Quick Bills do not deduct stock because no specific products are selected.
This is intentional — for a ₹20 packet of chips, manually selecting the product would slow things down.
Staff should use Full Bill for any item where stock tracking matters (expensive items, bulk purchases).
A monthly stock audit can account for the difference.

•  Computer Operator (1 person) — manages the screen, enters orders, confirms bills, prints
•  Floor Staff (everyone else) — takes verbal orders from customers, notes them on paper if needed
